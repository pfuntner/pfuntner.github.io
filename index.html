Gaming

I enjoy some online board and strategy games. Here are some useful links:

  • Cross Sums cheat sheet - I call it a cheat sheet but I suppose it's really not. It's just a list of all combinations of digits that will add up to a particular sum. I will use it when working on a puzzle to help me figure out cells in a puzzle. The pattern of combinations for a certain number of digits is very interesting! If you're not familiar with Cross Sums (aka Kakuro), check out the Wikipedia page. Personally, I've never heard it called anything except Cross Sums but it might be a regional thing. The USA is still using the imperial system rather than the metric system!

  • Hexversi axes labeling - ItsYourTurn has a great Reversi/Othello variation called Hexversi where the cells and the board are in the shape of hexagons. It's fun but the worse part is that the intersecting axes are diagonal and not labeled.

    This image attempts to label the axes:

    • The axes with letters (A through K) go from the upper left and upper edges to the lower and lower right edges

    • The axes with numbers (1 through 11) go from the lower and lower left edges to the upper right and upper edges

    I labeled three hexes as examples. Note that C6 and I6 are on the same long 6 diagonal. The other long diagonal is F
